Case Study of Sun Country Airlines

Portfolio

Introduction

Founded in 1983, Sun Country Airlines started as a charter airline, providing Minnesotans with winter escapes to sunny destinations. Despite challenges like ownership changes and economic pressures, the airline found stability in 2011 under the Davis Family and has since focused on leisure travel to warm-weather destinations and U.S. military charters.

Recent efforts have concentrated on enhancing customer engagement, advancing digital marketing strategies, and leveraging data analytics to optimize the Ufly Rewards program, improve the online booking experience, and foster stronger customer relationships in a competitive market.

Current Challenges

The current state of customer insights and relationship management is marked by significant deficiencies. Decision-making is primarily driven by anecdotal evidence due to the absence of comprehensive customer data. The organization lacks dedicated data analysts, and the available data sources are limited in scope and depth.

Although the website and backend systems have undergone partial upgrades, further improvements are necessary to fully meet customer expectations. Additionally, the Ufly Rewards loyalty program is underutilized, largely due to insufficient marketing efforts.

Strategic Objectives

To address these challenges, the organization aims to enhance its understanding of customer needs, behaviors, and preferences. Through data segmentation, the organization can develop more targeted and effective marketing strategies. Analyzing existing databases, such as the Ufly Rewards program and TSA flight records, will provide valuable insights and support more informed decision- making.

Furthermore, building data analytics capabilities is essential for sustained growth and competitiveness. A key objective is to enhance the online booking channels and overall digital experience, aligning them with the evolving expectations of modern travelers. This improvement is crucial for increasing both customer attraction and retention.

Another priority is to boost enrollment in the Ufly Rewards program by leveraging data-driven insights to optimize and promote the program more effectively. By harnessing improved customer insights, the organization can strengthen customer relationships, ensuring that the loyalty program becomes a central pillar in its strategy to foster long-term engagement and loyalty

Key Characteristics of Each Customer Segment

Group 0: Hub Loyal Travelers

Purchase products departing from MSP, the airline hub, and purchase products offered on the airline website. This group does not have many that opt for memberships, but it is a group that has a high probability of becoming a member if we use the right strategies to pull them in.




Group 1: The Traveling Tribe

This group of travelers usually buy round-trip flight tickets and plans their journey ahead of time 3 by making reservations in advance. They also tend to use third-party booking websites to purchase cheaper airline tickets. This group mainly travels with three or more people and primarily consists of Gen Z and Millennial couples in their 20s and 30s and Baby Boomers over 55 years of age.






Group 2 - The Lone Voyagers

These are the solo travelers that prefer traveling alone. These free roamers usually tend to travel during the peak vacation seasons. There is a tendency for these travelers to purchase one-way airline tickets as they are more flexible and spontaneous with their plans and crave the excitement and thrill of the unknown. This group tends to be more budget conscious and usually pack light for better mobility during their travels.




Group 3: Executive Frequent Flyers

This group includes flyers in their 30s to 60s, who have the highest loyalty membership status and travel a lot for the purpose of business. Most people in this group are business class travelers and many also upgrade to first class when they fly. This group tends to make reservations when needed rather than well in advance.




Group 4: Escape Adventurers

Most travelers in this group are middle-aged between the ages of 35 and 54 and might be more established in their careers. This group's demographic tends to travel in pairs or groups such as married couples who have raised children or have more independent children. This group is ready to travel as they have disposable income and more time on their hands to do so. The top destinations for travel for this group are Las Vegas, Cancun, and Florida as per our analysis as these are places with retreats, lavish resorts, eye - catching attractions, and leisure activities for relaxation.




Group Summary

Group0 Hub-Loyal Travelers

This group frequently purchases products departing from the airline hub (MSP) and tends to buy from promotions on the airline’s website. While they are not yet members, they have high potential to join the membership program if incentivized with targeted promotions.

Group1 The Traveling Tribe

This group consists of families who primarily purchase round-trip tickets and plan their trips well in advance. They often use outside booking platforms to find cheaper fares. The group typically travels in parties of 3 or more, and their demographic includes couples in their 20s and 30s with children, as well as grandparents over 55.

Group2 The Lone Voyagers

Solo travelers who tend to fly during vacation seasons (Q3/Q4), typically purchase one-way tickets instead of round-trip options. This group is driven by flexibility and spontaneity in their travel choices, often seeking out new adventures alone.

Group3 Executive Frequent Flyers

This group is heavily comprised of business travelers, aged 30-60, with a strong presence in the airline’s membership program. They frequently travel for work, often using first-class or upgrading to it, and tend to make last-minute bookings as needed for business purposes.

Group3 Escape Adventurers

This group consists mostly of 35-54-year-olds who prefer traveling with their partner or in small groups. Many are couples whose children are more independent, and they often travel to popular vacation destinations like Las Vegas, Cancun, and Florida.

Recommendations


Strategies for Hub-Loyal Travelers

Hub-Loyal Travelers customers frequently travel from a specific airport, often benefiting from the airline’s dominance at that location. A tailored loyalty program for this group can enhance their commitment by rewarding direct bookings and reinforcing purchasing habits through exclusive offers.

Firstly, airlines can introduce a "Join and Earn" initiative, offering a membership sign-up bonus that includes extra miles or exclusive discounts for customers booking directly through the airline’s website. Additionally, airlines can further enrich this offer by providing exclusive benefits, such as access to special meals only available to members. This encourages long-term loyalty by creating a sense of exclusivity.

Secondly, airlines can introduce "Hub-Exclusive Discounts," offering special fares for flights departing from key hub locations, such as Minneapolis-Saint Paul (MSP). This approach promotes loyalty to the specific hub by providing cost advantages to frequent travelers who consistently use the same airport. By aligning discounts with a traveler’s airport of preference, airlines can ensure that they remain competitive in retaining these high-value customers.

Finally, promoting "Website Only Deals" reinforces direct booking behavior. By offering promotions and discounts exclusively through the airline’s website, customers are incentivized to consistently use the airline's digital platform, enhancing both loyalty and customer engagement.

This strategy allows the airline to maintain better control over pricing while reducing dependency on third-party platforms.

Strategies for The Traveling Tribe

The Traveling Tribe consists of families and groups who prioritize value and convenience. Airlines targeting this demographic can enhance their appeal by offering bundled packages and early booking incentives that address the unique needs of family travelers.

A key offering for this segment is the "Family Bundle Discount," which provides savings for bookings of three or more passengers. In addition to the monetary discount, airlines can enhance the appeal by including value-added services such as free checked baggage or family seating arrangements. These benefits directly address the needs of families who seek comfort and convenience, thus promoting loyalty through enhanced travel experiences.

Furthermore, the "Early Bird Family Saver" offering special pricing or added benefits—such as free seat selection or higher mileage accumulation—when tickets are booked at least three months before departure, airlines can secure bookings earlier in the travel cycle. This not only helps families plan their trips in advance but also provides the airline with a more predictable booking pipeline, enhancing overall revenue management.

Additionally, airlines can introduce "Child-Friendly Packages," includes complimentary child meals and in-flight entertainment, ensuring that the travel experience is enjoyable for younger passengers. By addressing the specific needs of family travelers, airlines can build a reputation as a family-friendly option, further driving loyalty in this segment.

Strategies for The Lone Voyagers

Solo travelers, often seeking independence and adventure, represent another key segment for airlines. For this group, airlines can focus on offering promotions that appeal to the unique preferences and behaviors of individual travelers. The "Solo Adventure Package" provides a tailored bundle for solo travelers, offering discounted seat selection and free baggage. This package addresses the specific needs of solo voyagers by reducing costs and enhancing the travel experience, thus positioning the airline as a preferred choice for independent travelers.

In addition, airlines can release "Q3/Q4 Special Offers" targeting solo travelers during peak vacation periods. These seasonal promotions, which coincide with the busiest travel times in the third and fourth quarters, provide solo travelers with the opportunity to enjoy discounted fares and special benefits. Such timely offers capitalize on the increased demand during holiday and vacation seasons, encouraging solo travelers to choose the airline for their personal travel plans.

Though typically associated with families, "Child-Friendly Packages" can also benefit solo travelers who may be responsible for traveling with children. By offering child-friendly amenities such as in-flight entertainment and tailored meals, airlines can further enhance the customer experience for all travelers, improving overall satisfaction and promoting repeat business.

Strategies for the Executive Frequent Flyers

By offering customized corporate packages, exclusive perks, and loyalty-driven incentives, airlines can significantly enhance the overall travel experience for these distinct groups of passengers. One effective strategy for attracting and retaining Executive Frequent Flyers is the introduction of corporate discount packages.

Businesses that frequently book flights for their employees can benefit from bulk booking discounts, thereby reducing overall travel expenses. These corporate rates can be designed to offer incremental benefits based on the volume of flights booked by the company. As businesses increase their booking frequency, they would unlock greater discounts and additional perks, creating a compelling financial incentive for continued engagement with the airline.

Moreover, offering customized in-flight meal plans exclusive to corporate clients enhances the personalized nature of the travel experience. By aligning such offerings with the needs and preferences of corporate travelers, airlines can foster a sense of exclusivity and convenience, further solidifying their relationship with this customer segment.

Another important aspect of enhancing the travel experience includes access to dedicated concierge services and expedited security checks. Airlines can significantly reduce the time executives spend navigating the airport by offering seamless access to TSA Precheck, reducing the overall burden of travel for this high- value customer base.

Additionally, lounge access with amenities tailored to the needs of business travelers, such as meeting rooms and fast Wi-Fi, can enhance productivity and relaxation during layovers, thereby elevating their overall experience.

Strategies for the Escape Adventurers

Escape Adventurers, a customer segment focused on leisure and vacation travel, can be incentivized through specialized travel packages. Aim at couples seeking popular vacation destinations. By partnering with hotels and resorts in destinations such as Las Vegas, Cancun, and Florida, airlines can create comprehensive all-inclusive packages that cater to couples. These packages can include discounted flights, hotel stays, and additional perks such as spa services, ensuring a seamless and luxurious vacation experience for couples.

Additionally, providing these customers with attractive airfare discounts, such as "Buy One, Get One Half Price with double points" promotions, can incentivize them to travel more frequently. These incentives would not only appeal to the sense of value sought by leisure travelers but would also drive long-term loyalty to the airline.

Loyalty Status Upgrades for Escape Adventurers

Loyalty programs play a significant role in increasing customer retention, particularly among Escape Adventurers. Offering loyalty status upgrades based on the number of flights taken within a year can serve as a powerful incentive for increasing travel frequency.

Travelers who achieve higher loyalty statuses would gain access to exclusive offers and perks, such as complimentary upgrades or priority boarding, further enhancing their travel experience.

Such programs encourage travelers to book more flights, creating a cycle of loyalty and reward that benefits both the customer and the airline.

Conclusion

By analyzing the distinct behaviors and preferences of Sun Country Airlines' customer segments, targeted promotions can significantly enhance customer loyalty and drive increased bookings.

Implementing membership incentives, tailored discounts, and value-added services, such as family packages and corporate deals, aligns with the needs of each group. By focusing on personalized offers like "Join and Earn" for Hub-Loyal Travelers or "Romantic Getaway Deals" for Couple Escape Seekers, the airline can foster stronger connections with its customers, ultimately driving membership growth and increasing direct bookings.

These strategies, alongside seasonal and family-oriented promotions, will help Sun Country Airlines remain competitive, improve customer satisfaction, and optimize its marketing efforts.
